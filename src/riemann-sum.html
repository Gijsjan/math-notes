Riemann sum

Right Riemann sum

$$ \begin{align} startOfDomain &= a \\ endOfDomain &= b \\ numberOfRectangles &= n \\ nthRectangle &= i \\[3ex] rectangleWidth &= \frac{endOfDomain - startOfDomain}{numberOfRectangles} \\ &= \frac{b - a}{n} \\[3ex] rectangleHeight &= f(startOfDomain + rectangleWidth \cdot nthRectangle) \\ &= f(a + \frac{b - a}{n} \cdot i) \\[3ex] rectangleArea &= rectangleHeight \cdot rectangleWidth \\ &= f(a + \frac{b - a}{n} \cdot i) \cdot \frac{b-a}{n} \\[3ex] \text{sum of the rectangles} &= \sum_{i=1}^n \text{rectangleArea} \\ &= \sum_{i=1}^n f(a + \frac{b - a}{n} \cdot i) \cdot \frac{b-a}{n} \end{align} $$

Left Riemann sum

For the left Riemann sum, the rectangleWidth does not have to be added to the nthRectangle when calculating the rectangleHeight
$$ \begin{align} rectangleHeight &= f(startOfDomain + nthRectangle) \\ &= f(a + i) \\[3ex] \text{sum of the rectangles} &= \sum_{i=1}^n f(a + i) \cdot \frac{b-a}{n} \end{align} $$

Relation to integrals

$$\int_{a}^{b} f(x)dx = \lim_{x\to\infty} \sum_{i=1}^n f(x_i) \cdot \frac{b - a}{n}$$
